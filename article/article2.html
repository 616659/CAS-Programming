Second Article: Solutions to Environmental Problems

Chemical solutions:

Catalytic converters: elements such as palladium can act as a catalyst that removes harmful substances.
2CO(g) + 2NO(g) -> 2CO2(g) +N2(g)
Neutralization of acidic water: bases can be used to neutralize acidic water:
CaO(s) + H2SO4(aq) -> CaSO4(aq) + H2O (l)
Ca(OH)2(s) + H2SO4(aq) -> CaSO4(aq) + 2H2O (l)
Hydrodesulfurization: catalysts can be used to remove sulfur from fuels such as natural gas and petroleum which will reduce the sulfur dioxide emissions when fuels are burned

Personal habits/lifestyles:

1. Use degradable material


Plastics, styrofoams, glass and chemical batteries are an example of Non-bio degradable material.
It takes hundreds of years to break down and can have a detrimental effect on soil.
One way to reduce use of Non-degradable material is to start the 3Rs. Reduce, Reuse and Recycle.
For example, a milk bottle can be reused for different purposes before it goes into a rubbish bin.
And think twice before using disposable products. Using a tumbler instead of plastic & paper cups would be much more efficient.
Lastly, recycle as much as you can. Recycling aluminium cans can save 95% of the energy needed to produce new cans from raw materials.
Most of the products can be recycled. Throwing out products as rubbish will make more landfills.
Which affects the Earth’s ecosystem in a negative way. Try to be aware of the 3Rs and keep a sustainable lifestyle.

2. Save water


Many people have a habit of using water without any awareness.
Water is an essential source which is crucial for the ecosystem.
However, water isn’t unlimited. 71% of the Earth is covered with water and only 0.5% is available as freshwater.
And with 0.5%, humans use it for agriculture, drinking, industrial usage and sanitation.
It might be difficult to reduce water usage for agriculture and industrial usage.
Yet, people can change daily habits such as showers and tap water usage. Taking shorter showers can help reduce unnecessary water usage.
An average person uses 5 gallons of water per minute. If you manage to shorten shower time by 5 minutes, it reduces 25 gallons of water consumption.
Implementing a water saver at home can also help control water usage. Also, Check for leaks at home to prevent any leakage.
Overuse of water not only causes desertification but also water pollution. Chemicals used in factories and homes flow into the ocean, causing harm to marine ecosystems.
So start right now. Even a small change in habits like taking shorter showers can make a big difference.
Saving water isn’t just for humans, it’s for Earth.
