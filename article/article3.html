Third Article: Other Major Environmental Issues

1. Loss of Biodiversity:

Biodiversity refers to the variation in species, number of genes, individuals within species and the biological community in different regions.
The loss of habitat that leads to lack of food resources and pollution that leads to health problems in organisms are two of the causes of loss
of biodiversity. It is important to maintain biodiversity because lack of biodiversity can lower the food production in an ecosystem and create a
disturbance in an ecosystem. It also affects humans economically, as we rely on various organisms as food resources.

2. Climate Change:


Climate change is when there are major changes in the long-term pattern of weather. Greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide and methane are one of
the causes of climate change, they are released from human activities such as transportation and industrial activities. These gases trap heat in the
atmosphere, which leads to increased temperature. The effects of climate change include melting glaciers, increased natural disasters, sea level rise,
extinction and more. For instance, as the average temperature increases, the rate of water evaporation increased and led to droughts, which contributed
to more than $10 billion loss to agriculture

3. Waste Disposal:

Average person produces 4.3 pounds of waste per day and the amount of waste produced is overwhelming to the environment. As wastes are buried in landfills,
they can produce methane gas which again contributes to the greenhouse effect. Materials such as plastics are not biodegradable and they also release
toxins harmful to the environment.

4. Deforestation:

Deforestation is clearing forest and trees to use the land for other purposes. Such as, plantation, grazing and urbanization. Deforestation is a serious
problem in modern times. What makes it worse is that deforestation can cause other major environmental issues. First, climate change. Trees and plants use
carbon dioxide and water as main sources of energy. When it receives sunlight, it converts carbon dioxide and water into sugar and oxygen. Sugar is used
as energy and Oxygen is released into the atmosphere. According to ucsusa, deforestation is responsible for about 10 percent of carbon dioxide emission.
Continuing deforestation disables carbon dioxide purification and causes distraction in climate patterns. Carbon dioxide will likely increase due to the
growing population. Second, loss of biodiversity. Both humans and animals rely on forests. Rainforest only covers 7 percent of the earth but provides habitat
for more than half of the world living creatures(link). Forests also provide jobs for humans such as, plantation, grazing and mining. However, excessive
deforestation has been causing extinction of species, natural disasters and break down of the food web.
